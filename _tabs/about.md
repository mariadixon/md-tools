## Let Your Imagination Drive the Design, not Your Limitations.

Hi, I'm Maria Dixon. I build tools, frameworks, and strategies that make professional design accessible to everyone, because the path to becoming a designer shouldn't be gated behind a brutal learning curve.

As Director of Marketing at St. Andrew's School and a Digital Media instructor, I know what professional design demands. But more importantly, I know exactly what it feels like to stare at a program’s design space and have no idea where to start.

Here's the trap most beginners fall into: on one side are the drag-and-drop apps that promise instant results. They look great… until you try to make them yours. The layout breaks, the template falls apart, and even the premium upgrades don't quite fit. On the other side are the industry-standard programs that feel like sitting in an airplane cockpit before you've ever learned to fly. Most beginners take one look and retreat back to the limiting tools they already know.

## Why I Teach Adobe

I teach Adobe products because they are the commercial standard across the creative industry and my students at St. Andrew's have licensed access to the full suite. 

But here's what I always tell them: Adobe is just a tool. Design principles are the real skill.

Choosing a template because you like how it looks skips the foundation entirely. My goal is to graduate students who think like designers, not just operate software. Over years of teaching Digital Media and Marketing, I've watched students get genuinely excited looking at finished work, then visibly deflate the moment we launched an app. The blank workspace felt like a dead end.

So I started doing something different. Before opening any program, I gave them a pep talk, a real one, acknowledging that yes, it's going to look like a cockpit at first, and that's okay. Then I built tools to carry them past that moment. And now, those tools are available to you.
